PROJECTS CARRIED OUT
Rural Invasion 1.0 under Rising African Ambassadors
I founded Rising African Ambassadors with the vision to build Africa’s largest e-mentoring ecosystem that addresses the confusion of college graduates and dropouts and organizes their ideas. Essentially preparing them for a leadership path.
The junior arm of Rising African Ambassadors were the street kids who we referred to as our junior ambassadors – kids that loiter during school hours due to financial incapability. I got a team of four to embark on Rural Invasion 1.0 in Sokale village Abuja through an open application.
I was motivated to carry out this project on the account of a girl I met, Felicia by name, she is the 2nd second child in the family of five. I found her pick plastics during school hours
Out of curiosity we invaded the community for more insights. Sokale Village, is a small community of about 11,000 people known for hunting, hiking, farming and petty trade with bumpy road paths, they are surrounded by rocks and hills and our survey revealed that Felicia was in 3 out of every 5 homes we visited with majority being kids of farmers.
Their achools fees ranged from 5 dollars to 20 dollars, while some were kept out of school due to school kits that cost 2 to 4 dollars. For most families, they just need to feed atleast once in a day and ask God to help them rise from bed the next day.
We analyzed our data, placing these kids in three categories – out-of-school, sparingly in school and digital naivety. We also carried out Rural Invasion 2.0. We reached out to the United States Young Leaders Assembly and kept open communication with UNICEF. we are yet to get a comprehensive intervention program.
We haven’t given up on Sokale village whose population is 70% dominated by the age range of 15 to 25.The project hasn’t been closed, we identified 120 kids who would soon join the uneducated young adults masterminding unrest, havoc and cultism in the small community.
China-Africa loans Investigation under Revive Africa Initiative
In 2018, I volunteered for Revive Africa Initiative as a Strategic Communication Officer and Grant writer. Revive Africa Initiative is a not-for profit pan-African organization that wrestle against social injustice with wit while building the human capital of Young Africans.
In 2020, I was selected as a Nigerian representative to embark on an extensive research on China loans to Africa. We had the project for one month. Extracted our research materials from government officials, internet and some privy information from bush radios.
We came up with a comprehensive 30-page document that addressed the subject matter with practical recommendation for African Head of states for advocacy purpose.
